\begin{abstract}

In this paper, we present Verity, a graph-based framework for inferring source credibility from the structure of an information network. The information network is represented as a bipartite graph, where nodes correspond to sources and claims, and edges correspond to assertions linking sources to the claims they assert. Source credibility is estimated by applying an iterative propagation algorithm in which credibility is repeatedly propagated between sources and claims until a stable credibility distribution is reached. Unique properties of the proposed approach are explored in several experiments, which include convergence analysis, the initial allocation of credibility, graph connectivity effects, claim representation, and the incorporation of agreement-weighted propagation. It is shown that credibility can be estimated directly from the graph structure while allowing additional structural information to be incorporated into the propagation process. We believe that graph-based credibility inference provides a promising foundation for credibility estimation within the emerging Agentic Web.

\end{abstract}
